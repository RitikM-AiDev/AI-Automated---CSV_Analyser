📊 Chart Suggestions Based on Your CSV Data Analysis 📈

Important Note on Column Types:

Our analysis classified Order Date, Ship Date, Unit Price, and Unit Cost as Categorical. While these are typically Datetime and Numerical, our suggestions strictly follow the types identified in your output. Reclassifying these columns would unlock more powerful analytical charts, such as time-series plots or histograms for price distributions.

Suggested Charts by Analysis Type

I. Univariate Analysis: Exploring Individual Column Distributions

1. Histogram / Density Plot

  • Purpose: To visualize the distribution, shape, and spread of a single numerical variable.
  • Suggested Columns:
    • Units Sold
    • Total Revenue
    • Total Cost
    • Total Profit

2. Bar Chart / Pie Chart (Frequency)

  • Purpose: To show the frequency or proportion of each category within a single categorical variable.
  • Suggested Columns:
    • Region
    • Country
    • Item Type
    • Sales Channel
    • Order Priority
    • Order Date (Counts of orders for each distinct date string)
    • Ship Date (Counts of shipments for each distinct date string)
    • Unit Price (Frequency of items sold at each distinct price category)
    • Unit Cost (Frequency of items with each distinct cost category)

II. Bivariate Analysis: Uncovering Relationships Between Two Columns

1. Bar Chart (Categorical vs. Numerical Aggregation)

  • Purpose: To compare an aggregated numerical metric (sum, average) across different categories.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Region vs Total Revenue (sum/average)
    • Country vs Total Profit (sum/average)
    • Item Type vs Units Sold (sum/average)
    • Sales Channel vs Total Revenue (sum/average)
    • Order Priority vs Total Profit (sum/average)
    • Order Date vs Total Revenue (sum/average)
    • Ship Date vs Units Sold (sum/average)
    • Unit Price vs Total Revenue (sum/average)
    • Unit Cost vs Total Cost (sum/average)

2. Box Plot / Violin Plot (Categorical vs. Numerical Distribution)

  • Purpose: To visualize the distribution (median, quartiles, outliers) of a numerical variable for each category.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Region vs Total Profit
    • Item Type vs Units Sold
    • Sales Channel vs Total Revenue

3. Scatter Plot (Numerical vs. Numerical)

  • Purpose: To visualize the relationship, correlation, or patterns between two numerical variables.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Units Sold vs Total Revenue
    • Total Cost vs Total Revenue
    • Total Revenue vs Total Profit
    • Units Sold vs Total Profit

4. Grouped Bar Chart / Stacked Bar Chart (Categorical vs. Categorical Counts)

  • Purpose: To compare the distribution of one categorical variable across categories of another, showing counts or proportions.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Region vs Item Type (Count of orders/items)
    • Sales Channel vs Order Priority (Count of orders)
    • Country vs Item Type (Count of orders/items)

5. Heatmap (Categorical vs. Categorical - Aggregated Numerical or Counts)

  • Purpose: To visualize the strength of a relationship (e.g., counts or an aggregated metric) between two categorical variables using a grid of colors.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Region vs Item Type (e.g., sum of Total Revenue)
    • Sales Channel vs Order Priority (e.g., count of orders)
    • Country vs Item Type (e.g., average Total Profit)

III. Multivariate Analysis: Exploring Three or More Columns

1. Bubble Chart

  • Purpose: A scatter plot with an additional numerical dimension mapped to bubble size, and potentially a fourth to color.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Units Sold (X-axis) vs Total Revenue (Y-axis), with Total Profit (Bubble Size)
    • Total Cost (X-axis) vs Total Revenue (Y-axis), with Units Sold (Bubble Size)

2. Treemap / Sunburst Chart

  • Purpose: To visualize hierarchical data using nested rectangles or rings, where the size represents a numerical value.
  • Suggested Combinations:
    • Region > Country > Total Revenue (sum)
    • Item Type > Sales Channel > Total Profit (sum)
