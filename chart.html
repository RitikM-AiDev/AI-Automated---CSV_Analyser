CSV Data Analysis & Chart Suggestions

This document provides a concise analysis of your CSV data, suggesting various chart types to visualize your insights effectively. Understanding your data through charts can reveal trends, comparisons, and distributions at a glance.

Column Type Overview

  • Numerical: 5 columns, perfect for calculations and quantitative comparisons.
  • Categorical: 7 columns, ideal for grouping and comparing distinct categories.
  • Datetime: 2 columns, essential for analyzing trends over time.

Chart Suggestions by Purpose

1. Comparison Charts

Compare numerical values across different categories to spot differences and performance variations.

  • Bar/Column Chart

    Purpose: Compare a metric across distinct categories (e.g., Total Revenue by Region, Units Sold by Item Type).

  • Grouped Bar Chart

    Purpose: Compare multiple metrics across categories or break down a metric by a sub-category (e.g., Total Revenue and Total Cost by Item Type).

  • Box Plot

    Purpose: Visualize the distribution and outliers of a numerical variable across categories (e.g., Total Profit distribution by Item Type).

2. Time Series Charts

Track trends and patterns of numerical data over specific periods.

  • Line Chart

    Purpose: Show the trend of a numerical metric over time (e.g., Total Revenue over Order Date, Units Sold over Ship Date).

3. Distribution Charts

Understand the spread, frequency, and shape of a single numerical variable.

  • Histogram

    Purpose: Display the frequency distribution of a numerical dataset (e.g., distribution of Total Revenue, Units Sold).

4. Relationship Charts

Identify correlations and relationships between two continuous numerical variables.

  • Scatter Plot

    Purpose: Show patterns, clusters, and correlations (e.g., Units Sold vs. Total Revenue, Total Cost vs. Total Profit).

5. Part-to-Whole Charts

Illustrate the proportional contribution of each category to a total.

  • Pie/Donut Chart

    Purpose: Represent the proportional share of categories (e.g., Total Revenue by Region, Units Sold by Sales Channel).

6. Composition Charts

Visualize how different parts contribute to a total across various categories.

  • Stacked Bar Chart

    Purpose: Show the composition of a total numerical value segmented by another category (e.g., Total Revenue and Total Cost stacked by Region).

7. Geospatial Charts

When geographic data is available, these charts visualize data on a map.

  • Choropleth Map

    Purpose: Visualize numerical data geographically, coloring regions or countries based on aggregated values (e.g., Total Revenue by Country).

Important Note on 'Unit Price' and 'Unit Cost':

While classified as Categorical, if 'Unit Price' and 'Unit Cost' contain distinct numerical values, they can be treated as Numerical. This would enable additional powerful visualizations like Histograms for their distribution or Scatter Plots to explore relationships such as Unit Price vs. Units Sold, offering deeper insights into pricing strategies and profitability.
